<p><p>Single-Mode Optical Fiber (SMF) is designed for long-distance communication, allowing light to travel in a single path, reducing modal dispersion. The market includes ITU-T G.652, G.653, and G.655 specifications, each catering to specific applications. G.652 is standard for long-haul telecom, G.653 is optimized for wavelength-division multiplexing in dense networks, while G.655 supports non-zero dispersion-shifted fibers, enhancing performance for advanced communication systems. These varying types address different networking needs and performance criteria in the optical fiber market.</p></p>
