<p><p>The Vertebral Augmentation System market comprises several types, primarily kyphoplasty and vertebroplasty. Kyphoplasty involves the insertion of a balloon to create space in fractured vertebrae, followed by filling the cavity with bone cement, alleviating pain and restoring height. Vertebroplasty, on the other hand, directly injects bone cement into the fractured vertebra without creating a balloon cavity, providing stabilization. Other techniques may include methods like vertebral fusion or novel approaches currently in development, catering to diverse patient needs and conditions.</p></p>
