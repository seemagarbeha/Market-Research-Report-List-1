<p><p>The bicycle brake hydraulic fluid market consists of two primary types: mineral oil and synthetic oil. Mineral oil is derived from refined crude oil and is popular for its reliable performance and compatibility with many brake systems. It has a high boiling point and is less prone to absorbing moisture. Synthetic oil, on the other hand, is engineered for superior performance, offering better temperature stability and lower viscosity. Both types cater to different cyclist preferences and brake system requirements, influencing market dynamics.</p></p>
